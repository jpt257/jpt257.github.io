About

I am a PhD Candidate in Economics at the London School of Economics, working in development and environmental economics. My research examines the challenges non-governmental organisations face in programme delivery, the effect of climate change on the livelihoods of the global poor, and how credit constraints shape the demand for and effectiveness of workforce development programmes. My fieldwork is based primarily in Bangladesh and Uganda, and I am affiliated with STICERD and the Economics of Environment and Energy (EEE) programme.

Before starting the PhD, I worked at the US Library of Congress and in Bangladesh, where I contributed to research on poverty traps and the intergenerational transmission of poverty. I hold a BSc in Economics and Mathematics from Georgetown University, where I was awarded the Dahlgren Medal in Mathematics.
